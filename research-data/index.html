Comprehensive drought risk assessment and mapping in Taiwan: An ANP-ANN ensemble approach.

Liou, Y.-A.*, Vo, T.-H., Tran, D.-P., & Bui, H.-A.

Science of the Total Environment, 175835

SCIIF: 8.200RF: 31/358Environmental Sciences
Abstract

This study integrates ANP and ANN models to map drought risk in Taiwan using 20 indicators across hazard, exposure, and vulnerability dimensions. The model achieved an accuracy of 0.940 and was validated with ground truth data, demonstrating high reliability. The results confirm that this approach can rapidly and accurately predict drought risk under diverse scenarios.

Revealing the intricate relationship: Droughts and typhoons in Taiwan using the Standardized Precipitation Index (SPI).

Le, T.-V., Y.-A. Liou*, and K.-A. Nguyen

Journal of Hydrology: Regional Studies, Volume 55, 101917

SCIIF: 4.7RF: 17/127Water Resources
Abstract

This study confirms the close relationship between Taiwan's droughts and typhoon dynamics. Analysis reveals that the duration and track length of Western North Pacific typhoons influence long-term drought more significantly than typhoon frequency. Trends indicate that intensifying drought in central and southern Taiwan—versus decreasing dryness in the north—is linked to the concentration of typhoon tracks in northern regions. Furthermore, peak drought intensity typically occurs in late winter and early spring when typhoons are absent.

Four-decade spring droughts in Taiwan.

Vo, T.-H., Liou, Y.-A.*

Journal of Hydrology: Regional Studies, Volume 54, August 2024, 101849

SCIIF: 4.7RF: 17/127Water Resources
Abstract

This study analyzes spring drought in Taiwan, revealing that hydrological droughts respond more rapidly to rainfall deficits than agricultural droughts—a distinct deviation from global patterns. Since 2000, hydrometeorological droughts have intensified while agricultural droughts have declined. Drought hotspots are concentrated in central and southern Taiwan, exhibiting 4–6-year resonance cycles with temperature. These findings underscore the need for enhanced water resource management to safeguard the industrial economy.
